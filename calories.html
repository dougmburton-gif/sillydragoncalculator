How this is calculated

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation (1990), which studies have found predicts resting metabolic rate more accurately than older formulas for most people, within roughly a 10% margin for most users. BMR estimates the calories your body burns at complete rest; multiplying by an activity factor estimates your total daily energy expenditure (TDEE) — the calories you'd need to maintain your current weight. For weight loss, the NIH/NHLBI recommends a deficit of 500-1,000 calories per day for a safe rate of about 1-2 pounds per week.

⚠️ This tool provides general estimates for informational purposes only and is not medical advice. Individual calorie and nutrient needs vary — consult a doctor or registered dietitian before making significant changes to your diet, especially if you have a health condition.
