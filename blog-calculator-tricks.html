5 Calculator Tricks Every Student Should Know

1. Estimate before you calculate

Before you punch in the numbers, guess roughly what the answer should look like. If you're multiplying 48 by 21, you know the answer should land somewhere around 1,000 (50 × 20). If your calculator says 10,080, you know instantly something went wrong — probably a misplaced decimal or a mistyped digit — before you've written it down as your final answer.

2. Respect order of operations

A huge share of "calculator got it wrong" complaints are actually order-of-operations mistakes. Multiplication and division happen before addition and subtraction, and anything in parentheses happens first. If you're not sure how your calculator will read a long expression, break it into smaller steps or add parentheses yourself rather than trusting it to guess what you meant.

3. Use the memory keys for multi-step problems

Most calculators (including our scientific calculator) have memory keys (M+, M-, MR, MC). When a problem has several steps — like calculating tax and tip separately before adding them to a bill — storing an intermediate result in memory avoids retyping numbers and the copy errors that come with it.

4. Clear the display between problems

A leftover number from a previous calculation is one of the most common sources of wrong answers, especially on shared or borrowed calculators. Get in the habit of hitting clear (C or AC) before starting a new problem, not just when something looks off.

5. Double-check unit conversions manually

Calculators are great at arithmetic but don't know your units. If you're converting feet to meters or calculating a mortgage payment (see our mortgage calculator), sanity-check that the inputs are actually in the units the formula expects — a monthly rate typed in as an annual rate will still produce a number, just the wrong one.
