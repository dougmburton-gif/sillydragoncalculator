Farm Dog Breed Directory

Ten breeds commonly kept on hobby farms and homesteads — guardians, herders, and all-purpose companions — with notes on temperament, size, and the jobs they're bred to do.

Livestock Guardian

Great Pyrenees

Calm, watchful, and independent — bred to live outdoors with a flock and make its own decisions at night. Large (85–115 lb) with a thick weatherproof coat suited to cold climates. Needs secure fencing and space; not ideal for small suburban lots.

Size: Large · Coat: Heavy · Exercise: Moderate

Livestock Guardian

Anatolian Shepherd

A rugged, heat-tolerant guardian bred in Turkey to protect sheep from wolves. Fiercely loyal to its flock, aloof with strangers, and happiest with a job. Large (80–150 lb) and needs early socialization plus room to patrol.

Size: Large · Coat: Medium · Exercise: Moderate

Herding

Border Collie

Widely considered the most trainable dog breed — bred to control sheep with intense focus ("the eye"). Medium size (30–55 lb) but needs serious daily mental and physical work; under-stimulated Border Collies can become anxious or destructive.

Size: Medium · Coat: Medium · Exercise: Very high

Herding

Australian Shepherd

Energetic, intelligent, and versatile — herds livestock of all kinds and doubles as a devoted family dog. Medium (35–70 lb) with a striking merle coat. Thrives with acreage to run and a consistent job; not suited to a sedentary lifestyle.

Size: Medium · Coat: Medium · Exercise: High

Herding · Cattle

Australian Cattle Dog

Tough, tireless, and built to move cattle by nipping at heels — hence "Blue Heeler." Medium (35–50 lb), extremely high energy and drive, and can be nippy or headstrong without a job. A great fit for an active farm, a poor fit for apartment life.

Size: Medium · Coat: Short · Exercise: Very high

Herding · Small Farm

Pembroke Welsh Corgi

Short-legged but surprisingly capable cattle herder, historically used to drive livestock by nipping at heels while staying low to avoid kicks. Small (24–30 lb), big personality, and adapts well to smaller acreage than most herding breeds.

Size: Small · Coat: Medium · Exercise: Moderate
Shop Corgi supplies →
All-Purpose · Guard

German Shepherd

Versatile working breed used for herding, protection, and general farm duty. Large (50–90 lb), highly trainable, and forms a strong bond with one family. Needs consistent structure and daily exercise to stay balanced and non-reactive.

Size: Large · Coat: Medium · Exercise: High
Shop German Shepherd supplies →
Family & Farm Companion

Labrador Retriever

Friendly, food-motivated, and easygoing — less of a specialist worker and more of an all-around companion that tolerates farm chaos well. Large (55–80 lb), needs regular exercise to avoid weight gain, and gets along with most livestock and kids alike.

Size: Large · Coat: Short · Exercise: Moderate–High

Vermin Control

Jack Russell Terrier

Small (13–17 lb) but fearless — bred to go to ground after foxes and excels at keeping rodent populations down around barns and feed storage. Extremely high energy in a small body; needs a job and firm boundaries or it will invent its own.

Size: Small · Coat: Short · Exercise: Very high

Guardian

Rottweiler

Confident, powerful, and protective of home and family — descended from Roman drover dogs used to move cattle. Large (80–130 lb), needs early socialization and an experienced handler. A capable deterrent, not a breed for a first-time owner.

Size: Large · Coat: Short · Exercise: Moderate–High
