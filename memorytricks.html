Handy Memory Tricks

Mnemonic devices are little phrases that make hard-to-remember lists easy — the dragon collects the best ones here, for free, no calculator required.

Why mnemonics work

Your brain remembers a silly sentence far longer than a plain list. Turning "P, E, M, D, A, S" into "Please Excuse My Dear Aunt Sally" gives each letter a place to live in a story — that's the whole trick. Below are some of the most useful ones, for school, for trivia night, and for everyday life.

Math

Order of Operations

Please Excuse My Dear Aunt Sally

Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication, Division, Addition, Subtraction — the order to solve a math expression in.

Art & Science

Colors of the Rainbow

Richard Of York Gave Battle In Vain

Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Indigo, Violet — the order colors appear in a rainbow (and on a color wheel).

Astronomy

Order of the Planets

My Very Educated Mother Just Served Us Noodles

Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune — closest to farthest from the sun.

Navigation

Compass Directions, Clockwise

Never Eat Soggy Waffles

North, East, South, West — the four directions in clockwise order starting from the top of a map.

Geography

The Great Lakes

HOMES

Huron, Ontario, Michigan, Erie, Superior — the five Great Lakes of North America, in no particular order but easy to check off.

Calendar

Days in Each Month

Thirty days hath September, April, June, and November...

...all the rest have thirty-one, except February alone. The oldest mnemonic on this list, and still the fastest way to check a calendar.

Spelling

"Necessary"

One Collar, Two Sleeves

One "C", two "S"s — a shirt has one collar and two sleeves, and so does the word necessary.

Metric System

Metric Prefixes, Largest to Smallest

King Henry Died By Drinking Chocolate Milk

Kilo, Hecto, Deca, (base) unit, Deci, Centi, Milli — handy for converting between metric units without a chart.

Music

Treble Clef Lines & Spaces

Every Good Boy Does Fine · F-A-C-E

The lines (E, G, B, D, F) and the spaces spell "FACE" — everything you need to read sheet music at a glance.

Silly Dragon Original

Multiplying by 9

The digits always add up to nine

For 9 × any number 1-10, the two digits of the answer always add up to 9 (e.g. 9×4=36, 3+6=9). A fast way to check your work without the calculator.
