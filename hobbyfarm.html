Before you buy: check zoning first

What livestock you're allowed to keep depends entirely on your county or municipal zoning code — not on lot size alone. Some residential-agricultural zones cap the number of animals per acre, restrict certain species (roosters and livestock guardian dogs are common flashpoints with neighbors), or require setbacks between barns/coops and property lines.

Before closing on a property, ask the local planning/zoning office for the parcel's zoning designation and any right-to-farm or nuisance ordinances that apply. A realtor cannot substitute for this — zoning is public record and worth confirming directly.
